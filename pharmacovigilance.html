Post Graduate Diploma in Pharmacovigilance

Learn how to detect, assess, and prevent adverse drug reactions (ADRs) through real-world case studies, global regulatory frameworks, and hands-on safety report writing.

Patient Safety First

Understand how pharmacovigilance protects patients from preventable drug-related harm.

Global PV Framework

WHO, ICH, CIOMS, GVP — learn how international safety systems work together.

Hands-on ICSR Practice

Work on mock Individual Case Safety Reports using PV tools and real-world scenarios.

Career-Oriented Training

Mock interviews, resume support, and final certification to prepare you for PV roles.

Course Curriculum

Foundations of Pharmacovigilance

  • History of PV + major drug tragedies (Sulfanilamide, Thalidomide)
  • Understanding AE, ADR, SAE, SUSAR & medication errors
  • Basic PV terminology & safety concepts
  • Introduction to global drug safety systems
  • Practical: Mock adverse event reporting

Regulatory Safety Reporting

  • WHO, ICH, CIOMS & GVP guidelines overview
  • Hands-on: Filling ICSR (Individual Case Safety Report)
  • MAH responsibilities explained with case studies
  • Software training for ICSR processing
  • Practical: Real-life safety reporting simulations

Advanced PV Writing & Data Assessment

  • Causality assessment using WHO-UMC & Naranjo scales
  • MedDRA, COSTART & ART coding practice
  • Preparing DSUR, PSUR, PADER, PBRER reports
  • Signal detection & data mining basics
  • Practical: End-to-end PV case study project

Who Should Enroll?

  • B.Pharm, M.Pharm, Pharm.D & Life Science graduates
  • MBBS, BDS, BAMS, BHMS & other healthcare professionals
  • Freshers aiming for Drug Safety / PV careers
  • Professionals in Clinical Research / Medical Writing looking to upskill

Career Pathways

  • Drug Safety Associate / Pharmacovigilance Associate
  • ICSR Case Processor / Safety Data Analyst
  • Signal Detection & Risk Management roles
  • Aggregate Report Writer (PSUR, PBRER, DSUR)

This program is designed to make you job-ready for entry-level PV roles in pharma, CROs, and safety-specialist companies.

Program Format

  • Duration: 6–12 months (flexible depending on batch)
  • Mode: Online live sessions + LMS access
  • Schedule: Weekend / evening classes for working professionals
  • Deliverables: Assignments, case studies, project & final exam
